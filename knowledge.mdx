As you work through research, you build up knowledge — conclusions from experiments, insights from analysis, facts you've verified. Qualia's Knowledge system captures this knowledge as **claims** with clear provenance back to evidence.

## What are claims?

A claim is a durable fact or conclusion tied to your project:

- **Content**: The actual statement or finding
- **Sources**: Evidence supporting the claim (code cells, files, other claims)
- **Type**: How the claim was created
- **High-level flag**: Whether it's important enough to surface to agents immediately

Claims are project-scoped — they belong to a specific project and persist across sessions.

## Types of claims

### Notebook-captured

Created automatically when code runs and captures results:

- Variables marked as "required" on a task get captured
- The claim links directly to the notebook cell that produced it
- These claims mirror the evidence — when the cell changes, the claim is revalidated

### Synthesized

Written by agents to summarize understanding:

- Combine multiple sources (cells, files, other claims)
- Represent conclusions drawn from evidence

<Info>
  When an agent works on a task, it must create a synthesized claim before marking the task complete. This ensures every task produces documented knowledge.
</Info>

## High-level claims

Some claims are marked as **high-level** when they represent key findings. High-level claims are:

- Injected into the AI's context during generation
- Shown prominently in the Knowledge sidebar
- Filterable in the sidebar view

This keeps agents informed of your project's most critical knowledge automatically.

## The Knowledge sidebar

Open the **Knowledge** sidebar from the activity bar to browse your project's claims.

### Filtering and search

- **Type filter**: Show only notebook-captured or synthesized claims
- **High-level only**: Focus on key findings
- **Search**: Find claims by content or ID
- **Sort**: Newest/oldest, with high-level claims boosted

### Viewing claims

Each claim card shows:

- Claim content (truncated for long claims)
- Type indicator
- High-level badge if applicable
- Source count

Click a claim to see full details and navigate to sources.

### Views

- **List view**: Claims as cards
- **Graph view**: Visual DAG of claim relationships

## Working with claims

### Validation

Claims can be validated against their sources:

- **Validate**: Check if the claim still holds given current evidence
- **Validate upstream**: Check this claim and all claims it depends on

Validation runs a check — if sources have changed significantly, the claim may be automatically updated.

### Provenance

Every claim links to its sources:

- **Notebook cells**: Jump to the cell that produced evidence
- **Files**: Open the referenced file
- **Other claims**: Navigate the claim graph

This chain of evidence lets you trace any conclusion back to raw data and code.

## Claims and tasks

The knowledge system connects to [tasks](/tasks):

- Tasks require synthesized claims for completion
- Claims link back to the task that produced them
- The task graph and claim graph together document your research path

## Q_VARS integration

When tasks pass variables to downstream tasks via [Q_VARS](/q-vars), those values become notebook-captured claims. This creates a documented chain of data flow through your project.
